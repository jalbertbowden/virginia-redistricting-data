Population Estimates

Caveats and Explanations

CAVEAT: THE ATTACHED TABLES PROVIDE POPULATION ESTIMATES ONLY AND WILL BE REVISED BY THE 2000 CENSUS REPORTS DUE BY APRIL 1, 2001. THESE ESTIMATES ARE DESIGNED TO GIVE ONLY A PRELIMINARY PICTURE OF THE 2000 POPULATION FIGURES FOR VIRGINIA’S DISTRICTS, LOCALITIES AND PRECINCTS.

SOURCE OF DATA ESTIMATES

The Division purchased current estimates of demographic data for 1990 census blocks from Claritas, a private marketing firm. That data is the proprietary information of Claritas. The information provided by the Division of Legislative Services is aggregated data for precincts, localities, and districts.

FILE FORMATS

The tables are in Excel 5.0 format. The House#.xls and Senate#.xls files are about 25k in size each. The Prcts.xls file is about 400k in size. The Estimate.zip file contains all the tables and is about 200k in size.

METHOD TO ASSOCIATE CLARITAS ESTIMATES WITH CURRENT VOTING PRECINCTS, LOCALITIES, AND DISTRICTS

PRECINCTS: The Division combined the Claritas data based on 1990 census blocks with the Division’s geographic files for current voting precincts. That geographic voting precinct file is based on Census Bureau 1998 TIGER line files that have been revised substantially since the 1990 census blocks were created. Current voting precinct lines split many 1990 census blocks. In these cases, the Division assigned portions of the Claritas estimate for the 1990 census block to the current voting precincts that split that census block. These splits are estimates based on a visual examination of the block in question and the road features now shown by the TIGER files as lying within the block. An arbitrary percentage of the block’s estimated population has been assigned to each affected precinct. The populations of the split blocks ranged from 0 to more than 8,000. Many 1990 blocks in high-growth/new-growth areas had to be split to accommodate the current precinct lines.

LOCALITIES: Locality population totals were generated by totaling the populations of the current precincts in the locality.

DISTRICTS: District populations were generated by totaling the Claritas population estimates of the 1990 census blocks in the district. A limited number of 1990 blocks were split by district lines.

RELIABILITY OF DATA

The larger the area in question, the more reliable are the estimates.

The Claritas estimated state total population is 6,923,854. The Weldon Cooper Center provisional estimate for Virginia’s July 1, 1999, population is 6,872,900. The Bureau of the Census projection for Virginia’s July 1, 2000, population is 6,997,000.

The state total population under the Division estimates is 6,925,320 for precincts and localities and House of Delegates districts and 6,924,432 for Senate districts. The slightly higher Division state population totals exceed the Claritas figure by 1,466 and 988, respectively. These discrepancies result from translating the 1990 census blocks to the current geography files, assigning split block populations, and rounding numbers.

The faster growing localities in Virginia tend to show slightly higher populations under these estimates than the Weldon Cooper Center estimates which is a logical occurrence.

Population estimates for voting precincts are less reliable for several reasons:

    • the smaller area magnifies the impact of estimate and data entry errors;
    • a number of current precincts in the Division’s geographic file have been adjusted to follow acceptable census features and anticipated 2000 census block lines; and
    • assignments of the estimated 1990 block populations for blocks split by precinct lines have greater impact at the precinct level.
For these reasons, you will find in the locality and precinct table that there are a number of precincts showing a higher number of registered voters than the estimated voting age population. The number of registered voters in each precinct is taken from data provided by the State Board of Elections for the current precincts. Where the number of registered voters exceeds the estimated voting age population for a precinct, you may assume that the estimated population figure is low.

Claritas population estimates include 12 categories of population based on the 1990 census categories: six categories each for total population and for voting age population. The six categories are total, white, black, asian, indian, and other. No hispanic data is provided. Again, the reliability of the estimates is diminished for the smaller population segments.
